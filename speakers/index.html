Car Hacking with OpenXC

You can collect data on GPS data, engine speed (RPM), vehicle speed (kph), windshield wiper status, door open/close status, and more! With so many startups doing some recent fantastic work in the field of automotive transportation, and you’ll hear a snapshot of the exciting acquisitions, partnerships, etc that have been happening in the last few months.

Fireside Chat with John Werner

10:15pm - 10:45pm @ Media Room

The constant through John Werner’s career has been a passionate commitment to combining innovative thinking and collaborative, creative solutions to deliver human and environmental impact. Werner was most recently at the MIT Media Lab as Head of Innovation and New Ventures at the Camera Culture Group and Founding Managing Director of Emerging Worlds SIG. At MIT, Werner supported developments on new imaging platforms, including hardware and software technologies, to augment human vision. He is an accomplished triathlete and has qualified for and competed in the Ironman World Championship four times.

Computing the Magic - The Tech Behind Animation

11:00pm - 11:30pm @ Media Room

Gene Lee is a Senior Software Engineer in the Animation Technology Group at Walt Disney Animation Studios. He develops new tools and processes for creating both 2D and 3D feature films. Gene's film credits include "Tangled, " "Wreck-It Ralph," and the Oscar winning features, "Frozen," and "Big Hero 6." His recent projects include "Zootopia" and the upcoming feature "Moana."

Gene received an Sc.B. degree from MIT and a Ph.D. from the University of British Columbia, both in Computer Science. At MIT, he was a member of the MIT Media Lab and the Men's Lacrosse Team. At UBC, he was the president of the Computer Science Graduate Student Society, a member of the national champion Vancouver Lacrosse Team, and a two-time recipient of the ASI Exchange Communications Award.
